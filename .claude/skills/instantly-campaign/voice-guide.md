# Voice & Formatting Guide

Distilled from the user's winning Dentist campaign. Apply these rules to every email regardless of language.

## Voice rules

1. **First person, conversational, slightly disarming.** Write like you'd write to a friend who runs a small business. Not corporate, not chirpy.
2. **Hypothesize, don't claim.** "I figured you're probably losing X" beats "You are losing X". Hedging signals you're a human reaching out, not a script.
3. **One specific number, not a range of buzzwords.** "4-5 bookings a day" > "tons of revenue". If you don't have a number, hedge ("a handful", "nogle få").
4. **CTAs are always a soft question.** Permission-asking ("Må jeg sende den?", "Ville det hjælpe?", "Mind if I show you?"). Never imperatives. Never "book a call".
5. **Brevity wins.** No email is longer than ~6 short lines. If a line can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
6. **Reference a tangible thing.** Email 1 mentions an "example". Email 3 names a specific asset. People reply to artifacts, not pitches.
7. **No signature.** No name, no title, no company, no link block. Just the message.
8. **Pivot in email 5.** Don't keep beating the same angle. A second angle catches people the first one missed.

## Formatting rules

- Wrap every visible line in `<div>…</div>`.
- Use `<div><br /></div>` for blank lines between paragraphs.
- Use `<strong>` very sparingly — at most once per email, on a single phrase.
- No `<a>` tags, no images, no `<ul>`/`<ol>`, no fancy HTML.
- `{{companyName}}` only. No `{{firstName}}` or custom vars.
- Trailing space in the email-1 subject (`{{companyName}} `) is intentional — keep it.
- Subjects for emails 2, 4, 5 are empty strings (threaded replies).

## Do / Don't examples

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|------|
| "We help clinics increase revenue by 30%." | "Jeg tænkte at i sikkert går glip af 4-5 bookinger om dagen…" |
| "Book a 15-minute call here: [link]" | "Ville du have noget imod at se den?" |
| "Best regards, Albert — Founder, Shiney" | *(no signature at all)* |
| "Hope you're doing well!" | "Jeg havde lige lidt tid til at skrive til jer." |
| "Check out our amazing AI receptionist!" | "Jeg har lavet et eksempel der viser hvordan det kan se ud." |
| Long paragraph explaining the product | One sentence stating a hypothesis + one sentence asking permission |

## Language notes

- **Danish:** lowercase pronouns ("i", "jeres"), informal, minor typos OK (the original has "hælpe"/"anmeldelses" — don't over-polish; humans typo). Use "Jeg" / "i" / "jer" forms.
- **English:** keep it American-casual, lowercase-leaning, no em-dashes, no "I hope this email finds you well". Use "you" / "your team".
- **Other languages:** preserve the same brevity, hypothesis framing, and soft-question CTA shape.
